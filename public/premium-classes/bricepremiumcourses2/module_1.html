Module Overview

Welcome to Module 1: Financial Statements and Key Vocabulary. This module introduces the three core financial statements used in businesses worldwide — the income statement, the balance sheet, and the cash flow statement — along with the essential English vocabulary you need to read and discuss them confidently. You will learn how each statement is structured, what key figures mean, and how to talk about financial performance, liquidity, and profitability in a professional context.

This module is designed for finance professionals, accountants, analysts, managers, and anyone who works with financial information at B1–B2 English level. By completing all activities, you will be able to participate confidently in meetings, write reports, and discuss financial data with colleagues and stakeholders in English.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1
Identify and explain the purpose and structure of the three core financial statements: income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement.
2
Use key financial vocabulary accurately to describe revenue, expenses, assets, liabilities, equity, and cash flows in spoken and written English.
3
Apply prepositions of financial change correctly to describe increases, decreases, and movements in financial data.
4
Use the present perfect and past simple tenses accurately to report financial results and describe historical performance.
5
Form and use financial collocations and noun phrases that are essential for professional finance communication in English.
6
Interpret financial data from income statements and balance sheets and comment on a company's financial health using appropriate language.
7
Write a short financial summary report and a professional email reporting key financial results to a non-specialist audience.
8
Listen to financial briefings, earnings calls, and analyst presentations and extract key figures, trends, and recommendations.
