Module Overview

Welcome to Module 2: Understanding Balance Sheets. This module takes a deep dive into the balance sheet — one of the three core financial statements and arguably the most comprehensive window into a company's financial health. You will learn how to classify and describe every major section of the balance sheet, from current and non-current assets to short-term and long-term liabilities and shareholders' equity, using accurate and professional English.

Building on Module 1, this module goes beyond basic definitions to explore how analysts interpret the balance sheet — assessing solvency, leverage, liquidity ratios, and capital structure. You will practise the specific grammar and vocabulary used by finance professionals when discussing balance sheet items, trends, and what the numbers reveal about a company's strategic position and financial resilience.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1
Classify and accurately describe all major balance sheet line items — current assets, non-current assets, current liabilities, non-current liabilities, and equity — in professional English.
2
Explain the accounting equation (Assets = Liabilities + Equity) and articulate why the balance sheet must always balance, including in complex reporting scenarios.
3
Use comparative adjectives and language of contrast correctly to compare balance sheet figures across periods and between companies.
4
Apply reported speech and indirect reporting structures to summarise and present balance sheet findings from analyst reports and board briefings.
5
Form and use advanced balance sheet collocations and noun phrases correctly — including goodwill impairment, net tangible assets, and capital structure.
6
Calculate and interpret key balance sheet ratios — including the current ratio, quick ratio, and debt-to-equity ratio — and discuss what they indicate about financial health.
7
Write a formal balance sheet analysis report and a professional memo summarising balance sheet changes for a senior management audience.
8
Listen to and understand analyst briefings, investor calls, and finance training sessions focused on balance sheet interpretation and capital structure analysis.
