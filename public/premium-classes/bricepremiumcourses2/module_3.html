Module Overview

Welcome to Module 3: Income and Cash Flow Statements. This module takes a focused, professional look at two of the most widely studied financial documents in any organisation — the income statement and the cash flow statement. Building on the foundations of Modules 1 and 2, you will move beyond basic definitions to develop the language and analytical skills needed to interpret profitability, earnings quality, and cash generation in a sophisticated and accurate way.

You will learn how to describe every major line item on both statements, understand the crucial difference between profit and cash flow, and use the specific grammar and vocabulary that finance professionals employ when presenting, reporting, and discussing financial performance. This module is essential for anyone who works with financial results — including accountants, analysts, controllers, finance business partners, and senior managers.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1
Identify and explain every major line item on the income statement — from revenue and gross profit through to EBIT, EBITDA, and net profit — using precise and professional English.
2
Describe the three sections of the cash flow statement — operating, investing, and financing activities — and explain what each section reveals about a company's financial behaviour.
3
Explain the difference between profit and cash flow, including why a profitable company can run out of cash, and use the appropriate language to communicate this clearly.
4
Use conditional sentences (first, second, and third conditionals) correctly to discuss financial scenarios, projections, and hypothetical outcomes in professional contexts.
5
Apply concession structures (although, despite, even though, however, nevertheless) to contrast positive and negative financial findings in reports and presentations.
6
Form and use advanced income statement and cash flow collocations correctly — including revenue recognition, free cash flow, operating leverage, and earnings quality.
7
Write a formal income statement commentary and a professional memo analysing cash flow performance for a finance or management audience.
8
Listen to and understand earnings calls, analyst briefings, and finance training sessions focused on income and cash flow statement interpretation and performance analysis.
