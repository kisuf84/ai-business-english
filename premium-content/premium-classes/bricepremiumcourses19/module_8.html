Module Overview

Welcome to Module 8: Writing Sales Emails and Follow-ups. In modern sales, the written word is often the first — and sometimes the only — contact a prospect has with you before they decide whether to engage. A poorly written email can close a door that a well-written one would have opened wide. Yet most salespeople write emails as an afterthought, dashing off a few lines between calls without considering structure, tone, subject line psychology, or what the reader actually needs to see in order to respond.

This module builds on the prospecting skills from Module 2 and the pitch language from Module 3, applying them specifically to written communication. You will study the structure, language, and psychology behind the most effective types of sales email — cold outreach, post-meeting follow-ups, proposal cover emails, deal-nudge messages, and break-up emails. You will develop the grammar precision and vocabulary range needed to write emails that feel personal, professional, and purposeful in English.
