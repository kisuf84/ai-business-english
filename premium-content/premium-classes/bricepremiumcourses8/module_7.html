Reference Guide

🏛️   Case Studies

Real-World Cases

Explore Kotter's longitudinal research on 100+ organisations — the 60-70% failure rate, the eight failure modes corresponding to the eight steps, the three most consequential failures (insufficient urgency, coalition lacking power or conviction, premature victory declaration as the most insidious because it occurs when change appears to be succeeding), and his later work on the change narrative as the most underinvested success factor; and the specific cultural and structural dynamics that shape transformation in Latin American organisations — amplified middle management influence in high-power-distance contexts, relationship orientation as the most effective medium for change adoption, and the consistent finding that pre-announcement relationship building of 4-6 weeks produces higher adoption rates and lower resistance intensity.
