📋   Grammar Review

Reference Guide

Three reference boxes: keynote and public speaking structure — audience question before speaker agenda (hook types, what not to do, the audience-question test), sound bite construction (conclusion first, the 30-second test, the front-page test), and rhetorical devices (tricolon, anaphora, antithesis — with effective and failure examples); media interview disciplines — the message bridge (acknowledge/connect/deliver with genuine vs mechanical examples, frequency rule), handling hostile questions (pause/challenge/answer with false premise, double-bind, catastrophe examples), and crisis communication statement structure (empathy/accountability/facts/action/commitment); and advanced media strategy — proactive media strategy (journalist's story test, pitching language, off-the-record building, graceful declining), prepared spontaneity framework (argument familiarity not script, story in multiple versions, permission structures, the preparation paradox), and vocal authority disciplines (pace calibration, pause use, ending energy, filler words).
