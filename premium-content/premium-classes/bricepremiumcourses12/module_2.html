Module Overview

Welcome to Module 2: Interviewing Techniques and Vocabulary. The interview is the most consequential conversation in the recruitment process — and the one most susceptible to poor design, inconsistent execution, and unconscious bias. For HR professionals operating in English, the interview context requires a precise and specialised vocabulary: the language for designing structured questions, introducing a scoring framework, briefing an interview panel, managing a candidate's experience, redirecting an answer that is going off-track, probing for evidence, and communicating an outcome — both an offer and a rejection — with care and professionalism.

This module covers the complete language of interviewing from the HR practitioner's perspective — from competency frameworks and behavioural question design to the STAR method, interview scoring, bias awareness, and the verbal and written language of candidate communication. You will practise designing interview questions for specific competencies, evaluating candidate responses, and handling the challenging conversational moments that every interviewer eventually faces.
