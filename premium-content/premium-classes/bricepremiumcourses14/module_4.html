A Visit to the Post Office
▼

📖 Reading Text

On a Thursday afternoon, Paulo needed to send two items at the post office: a birthday card to his aunt in another city, and a package containing a book he had sold online. He had wrapped the book in bubble wrap and brown paper the night before. He arrived at the post office just after two o'clock. There were five people in the queue ahead of him. He took a number and sat on a bench near the window.

When his number was called, he went to counter number four. The clerk asked him what service he needed. Paulo explained both items. For the letter, the clerk weighed it and said a standard stamp would be sufficient – it would arrive in two to three working days. For the package, the clerk asked Paulo to fill in a small form with the recipient's name and address. The package weighed four hundred and twenty grams and the clerk gave him three options: standard, express, and tracked delivery.

Paulo chose tracked delivery for the book – it had been paid for by the buyer and he wanted to be sure it arrived safely. It cost three euros sixty. The letter stamp cost eighty cents. He paid in cash, received two receipts, and was given a tracking number for the package. He photographed the tracking number on his phone and sent it to the buyer immediately. He was out of the post office in twenty-two minutes.
