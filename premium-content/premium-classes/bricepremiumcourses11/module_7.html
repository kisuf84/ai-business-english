🏛️   Case Studies

Real-World Cases

Explore the ward that didn’t speak up — how an above-average mortality rate in the over-75 cohort was reported monthly in the IPR but presented with a contested case mix explanation the board accepted without challenge, while ward-level staff concerns about night medical cover and handover processes were raised informally but never reached the formal governance structures; a junior doctor’s Datix report and a family complaint eventually triggered a formal investigation; the findings identified four failures (data obscuring rather than illuminating the signal, poor escalation culture, dysfunctional divisional governance, and inadequate board challenge) and produced five systemic changes; and the OPEL 4 winter crisis — how a combination of influenza demand, reduced community care, and staff absences pushed the Trust to its highest operational pressure level with 47 ED patients awaiting inpatient beds, and how a structured response (CEO on site, elective cancellation, overflow opening, ICB mutual aid, ambulance diversion briefing) combined with three specific clinical interventions (MD-facilitated DTOC resolution, pharmacy discharge review, ITU bed release) reduced OPEL status to OPEL 2 within 36 hours — illustrating that capacity crises are managed by preparation, not improvisation.
