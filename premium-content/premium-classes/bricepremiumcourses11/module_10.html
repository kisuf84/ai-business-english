🏛️   Case Studies

Real-World Cases

Explore the NHS Bowel Cancer Screening campaign redesign — how screening uptake in the most deprived quintile (50%) significantly lagged the least deprived (78%), and how a COM-B analysis identified the primary barriers as capability (lack of confidence in completing the test), social opportunity (cultural taboo), and motivation (low perceived personal risk) rather than awareness; how a community co-design team redesigned the campaign with normalising step-by-step video, family-centred reframing, multilingual community endorsements, and one-to-one community health worker support; and how uptake rose from 51% to 64% in the intervention population at 12 months — illustrating that campaigns designed with the community, addressing multiple barriers simultaneously, outperform generic campaigns; and the risk communication failure and recovery around the MMR vaccine — how a fraudulent 1998 Lancet paper caused MMR rates to fall from 92% to below 80% in some areas, falling below the herd immunity threshold and causing measles outbreaks; how the initial official response was poorly handled (denials before evidence was understood, statements revised, anxiety not engaged with); and how the successful recovery involved five strategies (transparent safety data, scientific consensus, authentic community engagement, specific misinformation counter-narratives, and improved access) — illustrating that rebuilding trust requires sustained effort across attitudinal and structural dimensions, not a single message.
