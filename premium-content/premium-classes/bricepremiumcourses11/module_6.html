🏛️   Case Studies

Real-World Cases

Explore the opioid conversion error — how a foundation year doctor divided the individual morphine dose rather than the total daily dose when calculating an equivalent subcutaneous syringe driver prescription for Mrs Margaret Hartley, producing half the required dose; the patient suffered 24 hours of unnecessary pain before a palliative care nurse identified the error; the investigation found three root causes (inadequate training, no pharmacist review before administration, no conversion calculator in the EPR) and implemented three systemic changes (mandatory opioid conversion training, pharmacist countersigning for syringe driver prescriptions, integrated EPR conversion calculator) — illustrating that high-risk calculations require independent verification; and the single-use device reprocessing cluster — how reprocessing urinary catheters labelled ‘single use only’ using cleaning and chemical disinfection (inadequate for critical items under the Spaulding Classification) caused a cluster of healthcare-associated infections on a urology ward, triggered an MHRA investigation, and resulted in six patient infections — illustrating that ‘single-use’ is a regulatory classification with legal consequences, not a recommendation.
