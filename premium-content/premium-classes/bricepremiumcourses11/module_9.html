🏛️   Case Studies

Real-World Cases

Explore the report that was read — how a 47-page patient safety investigation report for a never event (wrong site orthopaedic surgery) failed to produce a useful governance discussion because it had no executive summary, no labelled recommendations section, and extensive clinical detail inaccessible to non-clinical board members; restructuring to 28 pages with a one-page executive summary, visual timeline, clearly labelled Findings and Learning/Recommendations sections (as a table with named leads and dates), and a Governance oversight section enabled the board to engage substantively with the five contributing factors and twelve improvement actions — illustrating that a patient safety report is only as good as its impact, and restructuring for the audience is itself a patient safety activity; and the audit that actually changed practice — how four years of sepsis pathway audits with vague recommendations (‘remind staff’, ‘provide training’) produced no measurable change (compliance 63%), while a fifth audit using element-level breakdown (blood culture compliance 38% vs antibiotic administration 81%), a specific mechanistic intervention (point-of-care blood culture request card on every drug trolley), and SMART action plan elements produced measurable improvement to 79% overall compliance within six months — illustrating that recommendation precision, not audit frequency, determines whether clinical audit drives change.
