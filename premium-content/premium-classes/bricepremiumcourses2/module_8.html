Module Overview

Welcome to Module 8: Writing Financial Reports. This module focuses on the language, structure, and style required to write financial reports that are not only numerically accurate but also clearly written, logically structured, and appropriate in register for their intended audience. Building on all previous modules in this course, you will now learn how to communicate financial information in writing — one of the most valued and least taught skills in professional finance.

You will study the range of financial report types produced in professional organisations — from monthly management accounts packs to board papers, investor updates, and annual report narratives — and develop the specific writing techniques, grammar structures, and vocabulary patterns that distinguish outstanding financial writers from average ones. This module is essential for controllers, FP&A analysts, finance business partners, CFOs, and any finance professional who writes reports for internal or external audiences.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1
Identify the purpose, audience, structure, and register of the main types of financial reports — management accounts, board papers, investor letters, and annual report narratives.
2
Apply the key principles of effective financial writing — clarity, concision, accuracy, objectivity, and appropriate register — in a range of professional report contexts.
3
Use the passive voice and impersonal structures correctly and purposefully in formal financial reports to achieve an objective and professional tone.
4
Apply nominalisation — converting verbs and adjectives into formal noun phrases — to achieve a more concise and authoritative register in financial writing.
5
Use discourse markers and paragraph connectors correctly to create coherent, well-structured financial report sections that guide the reader logically from one point to the next.
6
Write effective executive summaries that distil complex financial information into clear, actionable insights for senior decision-makers.
7
Write a complete management accounts commentary and a formal board paper section — using professional structure, tone, and financial language — for realistic business scenarios.
8
Listen to and understand workshops, peer feedback sessions, and expert discussions on financial writing quality, register, and common mistakes made by non-native English writers.
