Module Overview
B1–B2

Welcome to Module 12: Risk, Compliance, and Fraud Prevention. This module equips you with the specialised English vocabulary and communication skills needed to engage with enterprise risk management, regulatory compliance obligations, and corporate fraud prevention frameworks. From identifying and categorising financial risks, to drafting risk registers and presenting compliance reports, to discussing anti-money laundering controls and whistleblower procedures — this module covers the language professionals need to operate at the intersection of finance, law, and governance. Whether you work in risk, audit, compliance, or general finance, the skills developed here will enable you to communicate with confidence and authority on topics that are increasingly central to modern corporate life.

🎯 Learning Outcomes

  • Identify and correctly use key risk, compliance, and fraud prevention vocabulary in professional contexts
  • Read and interpret formal texts on enterprise risk management frameworks, financial crime, and regulatory obligations
  • Discuss risk registers, compliance failures, fraud indicators, and control responses using precise professional English
  • Understand and produce relative clauses — defining and non-defining — in risk and compliance documentation
  • Use prepositional phrases of cause, manner, and purpose accurately in regulatory and risk writing
  • Apply nominalisation to transform verb-heavy language into formal noun-phrase structures typical of compliance reports
  • Listen to and comprehend spoken English in risk committee meetings, compliance briefings, and fraud investigation scenarios
  • Write a formal risk assessment memo and an anti-fraud policy statement
  • Analyse real-world risk management and fraud case studies
