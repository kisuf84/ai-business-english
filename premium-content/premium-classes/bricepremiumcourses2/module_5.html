Module Overview

Welcome to Module 5: Financial Ratios and Performance Indicators. This module provides a comprehensive, professionally oriented guide to the financial ratios used by analysts, investors, credit officers, and finance professionals to assess business performance. Building on the financial statement knowledge of Modules 1–4, you will now learn how to calculate, contextualise, and communicate key ratios across five major categories: profitability, liquidity, efficiency, leverage, and market valuation.

This module is designed for professionals who need to present ratio analysis in meetings and reports, interpret ratios in analyst briefings and credit assessments, and use the precise English vocabulary and grammar structures that make ratio analysis credible, clear, and persuasive. You will practise the specific language patterns used when ratios are improving, deteriorating, or being compared across companies and time periods.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1
Calculate and explain the key profitability ratios — gross margin, EBITDA margin, net profit margin, return on equity, and return on capital employed — in professional English.
2
Calculate and interpret liquidity ratios — current ratio and quick ratio — and efficiency ratios — inventory days, receivable days, and payable days — and explain what they reveal about operational performance.
3
Describe and contextualise leverage ratios — net debt to EBITDA and interest cover — using precise language to discuss financial risk and debt sustainability.
4
Explain the main market valuation ratios — P/E ratio, EV/EBITDA, and price-to-book — and discuss what they imply about investor expectations and company value.
5
Use noun clauses and relative clauses correctly to define, explain, and contextualise financial ratios in written and spoken professional communication.
6
Apply language of cause and effect accurately to explain why ratios have improved or deteriorated and what they imply for management action.
7
Write a formal ratio analysis report comparing two companies and a professional briefing note interpreting a company's key performance indicators for a non-specialist audience.
8
Listen to and understand analyst briefings, investor presentations, and credit assessments that use financial ratios to evaluate business performance and investment risk.
