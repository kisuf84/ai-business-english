🏛️   Case Studies

Real-World Cases

Explore the concierge who changed the hotel’s reputation — how one hotel’s programme of requiring all front desk and concierge staff to visit twelve local attractions, keep notes records, share monthly discoveries, and compete in local knowledge quizzes transformed its TripAdvisor ‘concierge and local knowledge’ subcategory score from below-average to the highest in its competitive set, with staff named in 43 reviews (up from 7) and a 6% occupancy improvement; and the recommendation that went wrong — how a generic time-pressured recommendation of Kemble Castle to a couple without asking about accessibility sent a guest with a recent hip replacement to a 200-step uneven terrain visit, and how the duty manager’s exemplary recovery — acknowledging the failure, redesigning the remaining two days, calling ahead to Thornton House, and arranging a hotel car loan — resulted in a five-star review specifically praising the recovery.
