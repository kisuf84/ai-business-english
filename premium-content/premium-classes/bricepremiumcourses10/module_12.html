Real-World Cases

Explore the evacuation that saved lives — how a well-prepared hotel team’s systematic training (all fire marshals trained within six months, PEEPs in place for mobility-impaired guests, address pinned to the front desk phone) evacuated 104 guests in seven minutes from a serious kitchen fire with zero injuries, with the fire service commander specifically commending the speed of evacuation, the quality of the muster point register, and the immediate and accurate information provided on arrival; and the incident that should have been prevented — how three informal reports from housekeeping over six months about a damp second-floor corridor near the lift were never formally recorded, no corrective action was taken, and a guest subsequently fractured her hip in a fall, resulting in a £175,000 personal injury settlement, an HSE improvement notice, and a total incident cost of over £200,000 against a £300 window seal repair that could have prevented it all.
