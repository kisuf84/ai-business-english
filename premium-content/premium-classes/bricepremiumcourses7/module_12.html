🏭   Case Studies

Real-World Cases

Explore how the Toyota Production System was built incrementally by direct observation rather than academic theory — its two pillars (jidoka: stop when abnormal; just-in-time: produce only what is needed) and the human element of respect for operator knowledge that gives the tools their power; and how Six Sigma was developed at Motorola, dramatically amplified by GE's Jack Welch who required Belt certification for management advancement, and how its DMAIC methodology's distinctive features (statistical rigour in measurement, mandatory control systems) separated it from less rigorous improvement approaches — along with the measurement dysfunction critique that applies when organisations count Belt certifications rather than operational improvements.
