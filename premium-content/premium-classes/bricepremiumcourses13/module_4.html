My Favorite Shop
▼

📖 Reading Text

My favourite shop is a small bookshop called 'Page & Ink'. It is on a quiet side street in the city centre, between a café and a flower shop. The shop has a green wooden door and a small sign above the entrance. When you open the door, a little bell rings. Inside, the walls are covered from floor to ceiling with shelves full of books. The shop smells of old paper and wood.

The owner of Page & Ink is an older man called Mr. Ferris. He has grey hair and round glasses and always wears a cardigan. He knows where every book is in the shop. If you tell him what kind of story you enjoy, he will find the perfect book for you in less than two minutes. He has recommended many excellent books to me over the years.

I go to Page & Ink almost every month. I usually spend about thirty minutes looking around before choosing something. I love the feeling of picking up a book, reading the back cover, and imagining the story inside. Last month, Mr. Ferris recommended a novel about a young explorer. I read it in four days and loved it. I think independent bookshops like Page & Ink are very special and important. I hope they never disappear.

❓ Comprehension Questions

1. What is the bookshop called?

2. Where is the shop located?

3. What happens when you open the door?

4. What does the shop smell of?

5. How does the owner help customers?

6. How long does the writer spend in the shop?

7. What did Mr. Ferris recommend last month?

8. How long did the writer take to read the recommended novel?

📖 Reading Text

My favourite street is called Maple Avenue. It is about ten minutes' walk from my house. The street is lined on both sides with large maple trees. In autumn, the leaves turn bright orange, red, and yellow. Walking along Maple Avenue in October feels like walking through a painting. The ground is covered with fallen leaves that crunch under your feet.

Maple Avenue has some nice small shops and cafés. There is a family-run bakery called 'Morning Rise' that bakes fresh bread every day. The smell of the bread reaches the street in the morning. There is also a small florist, a second-hand bookshop, and an ice cream parlour that is always busy in summer. At the end of the street, there is a small square with a fountain.

I walk along Maple Avenue at least once a week. Sometimes I go alone and think quietly. Sometimes I go with friends. On Sunday mornings, the street is calm and peaceful. There are not many cars. People walk their dogs and buy fresh bread from the bakery. I know some of the shop owners by name. Maple Avenue feels like a village within a city. It is my favourite place for a slow, peaceful walk.

3. What do the trees look like in autumn?

My Favorite Gift
▼

📖 Reading Text

The best gift I have ever received was a telescope. My parents gave it to me for my thirteenth birthday. I had always been fascinated by space and the night sky. I had a poster of the solar system on my bedroom wall and had read many books about astronomy. When I opened the box and saw the telescope, I could not speak. I was completely speechless.

The telescope is not enormous, but it is very good quality. That same night, my father helped me set it up in the garden. We waited until it was completely dark. I looked through the eyepiece and saw the moon in incredible detail. I could see the craters and the shadows along the edge. I also saw Jupiter and two of its moons. I stood in the garden for two hours, looking up.

Since that birthday, I have used the telescope almost every clear night. I keep a notebook where I record what I see: the date, the weather, and a small drawing of what I observed. My best observation so far was a meteor shower last November. I counted forty-seven meteors in two hours. The telescope has deepened my love of astronomy and made me want to study it at university one day.

❓ Comprehension Questions

1. What was the best gift the writer ever received?

2. Who gave it as a gift?

3. For which birthday was the gift?

4. What did the writer see on the moon?

5. What planet did the writer also see?

6. How long did the writer stand in the garden that first night?

7. What does the writer record in the notebook?

8. What was the best observation so far?

No school, no worries, just the sea, my family, and the simple pleasure of fishing and walking on the beach. My parents say I talked about that holiday for years afterwards. Now, whenever I feel stressed or tired, I close my eyes and imagine sitting on that pier, feeling the warm morning sun on my face.

Making My Bed
▼

📖 Reading Text

Every morning, the first thing I do after getting up is make my bed. My mother taught me to do this when I was seven. At first I found it boring and unnecessary. Why make the bed if I am only going to get back into it in the evening? But my mother explained that making the bed sets the tone for the day. It is a small task that gives you a sense of order and accomplishment right at the start.

I have a double bed with a duvet, two pillows, and a bedspread. First, I straighten the fitted sheet. Then I shake the duvet and spread it evenly over the bed. I fold the top of the duvet back about thirty centimetres. I place the two pillows symmetrically at the head. Finally, I smooth out any wrinkles with my hands. The whole process takes about two minutes.

My bed-making has improved a lot over the years. When I was younger, it was messy and uneven. Now it looks quite neat. My mother says it looks hotel-quality. I do not know about that, but I do know that walking into a tidy bedroom with a made bed feels much better than walking into a messy one. I have not missed a single morning of bed-making in the last two years.

❓ Comprehension Questions

1. What is the first thing the writer does after getting up?

2. At what age was the writer taught to make the bed?

3. Why did the writer find it boring at first?

4. What does making the bed do, according to the mother?

5. How far does the writer fold the top of the duvet back?

6. How long does making the bed take?

7. What does the mother say about the bed-making now?

8. How long has the writer not missed a single morning?

A Day Without Electricity
▼

📖 Reading Text

Last winter, there was a power cut in our neighbourhood. It happened on a Sunday afternoon at about three o'clock. The lights went out and all the screens in the house went dark. The heating, which runs on electricity, also stopped working. My father called the electricity company and they said the power would be restored by eight o'clock in the evening.

At first, everyone in my family was a little lost. We are so used to having electricity that we did not know what to do. My mother lit several candles around the living room. We all put on extra jumpers and blankets. My father found a battery-powered radio in a cupboard. We sat together and listened to music. My sister and I played cards by candlelight.

By seven o'clock, the house was quite cold. My mother made hot soup on our gas cooker – fortunately, the cooker runs on gas, not electricity. We ate together by candlelight. It felt unusual and almost magical. At eight fifteen, the lights suddenly came back on. Everyone cheered. But for a moment, before turning everything back on, I thought about how much we depend on electricity every single day. It was a good reminder to appreciate what we usually take for granted.

❓ Comprehension Questions

1. When did the power cut happen?

2. What else stopped working besides the lights?

3. What time did the electricity company say power would be restored?

4. What did the mother do for light?

5. What did the father find in a cupboard?

6. What did the writer and sister do?

7. Why could the mother still make soup?

8. What time did the electricity come back?

Going to bed in a warm room while it was freezing outside felt like the greatest luxury. I actually enjoyed the cold day.

▼

📖 Reading Text

Last August, we had one of the hottest days I can remember. The temperature reached thirty-seven degrees. Going outside was unpleasant. The sun was very strong and there was no wind. My family decided to stay home and keep the house as cool as possible. We closed all the curtains and shutters on the sunny side of the house to block the heat.

My mother filled a large bowl with cold water and ice cubes. She put it on the kitchen table with a selection of cold drinks. We all walked around in as few clothes as possible. My sister and I ran through the water. We had a simple cold dinner – salad, cheese, fruit, and bread. No cooking was needed or wanted. We sat outside until ten at night watching the stars. Despite the extreme heat, it turned out to be a very pleasant and unusual day at home.

▼

📖 Reading Text

My favourite school activity is the Friday afternoon debate class. Once a month, our class has a structured debate on a topic chosen by the teacher. The class is divided into two teams. One team argues in favour of the topic and the other argues against it. You are sometimes assigned a position you do not personally agree with, which makes it more challenging and interesting.

Last month, the debate topic was: 'Smartphones should be banned in schools.' I was on the team arguing in favour of the ban. Personally, I am not sure about this, but I had to argue strongly for it. My team prepared our points: smartphones are distracting, they cause students to stop paying attention, and they can be used to cheat in tests. We also prepared answers to the other team's likely arguments.

The debate itself was lively and fun. The other team made some good points, especially about smartphones being useful for research. But our team stayed calm and responded clearly. The teacher judged the debate and said both teams argued well. She said my contribution was particularly clear and well-organised. Debate class teaches me to think quickly, listen carefully, and express myself with confidence. It is my favourite hour of the school week.

❓ Comprehension Questions

1. How often is the debate class?

2. What was last month's debate topic?

3. Which side was the writer on?

4. Which was NOT one of the writer's team's arguments?

5. What strong point did the other team make?

6. Who judged the debate?

7. What did the teacher say about the writer's contribution?

8. What skills does debate class teach?

My Favorite Classroom Activity
▼

📖 Reading Text

My favourite classroom activity is a creative writing task we do in English class called 'Five-Minute Stories'. Our teacher gives us a prompt – a first sentence, a picture, or a single word – and we have exactly five minutes to write a short story. There are no rules about length or genre. The only requirement is to write continuously and not stop to think too long.

I love this activity because it frees me from perfectionism. When I have a longer writing assignment, I sometimes freeze because I want every sentence to be perfect before moving on. In the Five-Minute Story, there is no time for that. You just write. Some of my best ideas have come from these five-minute bursts. Last week, the prompt was the word 'mirror'. I wrote a story about a girl who could see her future in a mirror. My teacher read it to the class.

Five-Minute Stories have improved my writing in general. I am now quicker to start, more willing to take risks, and less afraid of imperfection. My teacher says the goal is to unlock the imagination, not to produce perfect grammar. Once you have the ideas, you can improve the writing later. I agree. The activity has made writing feel playful and exciting instead of daunting.

❓ Comprehension Questions

1. What is the activity called?

2. What is the prompt?

3. How long do students have to write?

4. What is the only requirement?

5. Why does the writer love this activity?

6. What was last week's prompt?

7. What happened to the writer's story?

8. What does the writer say writing has become?
