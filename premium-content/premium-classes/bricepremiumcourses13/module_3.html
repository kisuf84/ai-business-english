My Favorite Drink
▼

📖 Reading Text

My favorite drink is freshly squeezed orange juice. I love it because it tastes sweet and refreshing. I drink a glass every morning with my breakfast. My mother buys oranges at the market every Saturday. We have a small electric juicer in the kitchen. Making fresh juice takes only a few minutes and it tastes much better than juice from a carton.

In summer, I drink orange juice with ice cubes. It is very cold and delicious on hot days. Sometimes I add a little mint from the garden. This makes the juice even more refreshing. My friends like it when I make juice for them. My friend Tomas said it is the best juice he has ever tasted.

Orange juice is also very healthy. It has a lot of vitamin C, which is good for the immune system. My mother says drinking orange juice every day helps prevent colds. I rarely get sick, and I think it is partly because of the orange juice. In winter, I also drink warm drinks like tea and hot chocolate, but orange juice is always my number one. I cannot imagine breakfast without it.

❓ Comprehension Questions

1. What is the writer's favorite drink?

2. When does the writer drink it?

3. Where does the mother buy the oranges?

4. What does the writer add to the juice in summer?

5. What did Tomas say about the juice?

6. What vitamin does orange juice contain?

7. What does the mother say orange juice helps prevent?

8. What warm drinks does the writer mention for winter?

📖 Reading Text

My favourite English word is 'serendipity'. It means finding something good or beautiful by accident, without looking for it. For example, if you are walking in the park and you suddenly find a beautiful flower you did not expect, that is serendipity. I first heard this word in an English lesson when I was twelve years old. My teacher wrote it on the board and asked us what we thought it meant.

I love this word because it sounds beautiful when you say it out loud. It has five syllables: se-ren-dip-i-ty. The sound is soft and musical. It is one of those words that sounds exactly like what it means. When I say it, I think of happy surprises, lucky moments, and pleasant discoveries. It makes me feel good just to say it.

Since I learned this word, I have noticed serendipity in my own life. Last week, I was looking for a book in the library and I accidentally found a book about photography that I loved. That was serendipity. I try to write down my favourite English words in a special notebook. I have about thirty words so far. Learning new words makes me feel like I am collecting treasures.

❓ Comprehension Questions

1. What is the writer's favourite English word?
