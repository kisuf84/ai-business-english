Last Saturday, I went shopping with my friend Paula. We went to the shopping centre. Paula wanted to buy a birthday present for her mother. We went to several shops. First, we went to a perfume shop. We tested different perfumes. Paula found a nice floral perfume that her mother would like. It was a little expensive, but Paula saved her pocket money for three weeks.

After buying the present, we were hungry. We went to the food court on the top floor of the shopping centre. There were many different types of food: pizza, sushi, burgers, and salads. We chose a Mexican food stand. I ordered a burrito and Paula ordered chicken tacos. The food was very good and not too expensive. We sat and talked for a long time. We had a great afternoon.

❓ Comprehension Questions

2. What does the writer enjoy sometimes without buying?

3. Who did the writer go shopping with last Saturday?

4. What did Paula want to buy?

5. What type of present did Paula buy?

6. How did Paula save money for the gift?

7. Where did they eat?

8. What did the writer order at the food stand?

Visiting a Museum
▼

📖 Reading Text

Last Sunday, my school organised a voluntary trip to the city history museum. I decided to go because I like history. Twelve students from my class joined the trip. We went by public transport. The museum is in the old part of the city, near the cathedral. It is inside a beautiful old building that used to be a palace.

Inside the museum, there were six permanent exhibition rooms. The first room was about the ancient history of the city. There were old coins, tools, and pottery. In the second room, there were medieval weapons and armour. I found it very interesting. The third room had paintings and photographs from the nineteenth century. The museum also had a special temporary exhibition about traditional music.

We spent about two hours in the museum. A guide explained the main exhibits to us. She was very knowledgeable and made history come alive. At the end, I bought a small book about the city's history in the museum shop. It cost five euros. When I got home, I started reading it immediately. I am now even more interested in the history of our city. I plan to come back to the museum with my parents.

❓ Comprehension Questions

1. Why did the writer decide to go on the trip?

2. How many classmates joined the trip?

3. Where is the museum?

4. What was in the first exhibition room?

5. What did the writer find most interesting?

6. How long did they spend in the museum?

7. What did the writer buy at the museum shop?

8. What does the writer plan to do?

A Day at the Beach
▼

📖 Reading Text

Last August, my family spent a day at the beach. We went to a beach about an hour from our city. We left home early in the morning to avoid traffic. We arrived at the beach at nine thirty. It was already sunny and warm. We found a good spot and set up our umbrella and towels on the sand.

The beach was beautiful. The water was clear and blue. My younger brother and I went straight into the sea. The water was cool and refreshing. We swam, played in the waves, and built sandcastles on the shore. My parents relaxed under the umbrella. My father read his book and my mother slept for a while.

At lunchtime, we ate sandwiches and drank cold water and juice that we brought from home. In the afternoon, we hired surfboards and tried surfing. It was difficult but very funny. I fell off the board many times. My father was surprisingly good at it. At six o'clock, we packed up our things and drove home. We stopped at a small restaurant on the way home and had fish and chips. It was a perfect summer day.

2. What time did they arrive at the beach?

3. What did the father do at the beach?

4. What did the brother and writer do in the sea?

5. What did they eat for lunch?

6. What did they hire in the afternoon?

7. Who was surprisingly good at surfing?

8. What did they eat on the way home?

My Favourite Holiday
▼

📖 Reading Text

My favourite holiday was a trip to Portugal two summers ago. We flew from our city to Lisbon. The flight was two hours. Lisbon is the capital of Portugal. It is a beautiful city with colourful buildings, hills, and a wide river called the Tagus. We stayed in Lisbon for three days and then we drove south to the Algarve coast.

In the Algarve, we stayed in a small hotel near the sea for five days. The beach was stunning. The sand was golden and the water was warm and clear. We swam every day. We visited several small fishing villages. We ate grilled fish and fresh seafood at local restaurants. The food was excellent. I tried fresh sardines for the first time. They were delicious.

On the last day, we visited a dramatic cliff called Ponta da Piedade. The orange and yellow rocks and the blue sea were breathtaking. My father took many photos. On the way to the airport, we bought souvenirs: a small ceramic tile and some traditional sweets called pastéis de nata. I still have the ceramic tile in my bedroom. When I see it, I remember that wonderful holiday. I hope to return to Portugal one day.

❓ Comprehension Questions

1. When did the writer go to Portugal?

2. How long was the flight to Lisbon?

3. What river is in Lisbon?

4. How many days did they stay in the Algarve?

5. What food did the writer try for the first time?

6. What is Ponta da Piedade?

7. What souvenir did the writer buy?

8. Where is the ceramic tile now?
